About

Dr. Katie Davis is a licensed clinical psychologist in New York State. She maintains a clinical practice on the Upper East Side of Manhattan as part of the Brooklyn Learning Center providing psychotherapy to children experiencing academic difficulties.

Dr. Davis practices using a neuropsychological treatment modality for people with learning disorders that integrates psychotherapy with cognitive remediation and special education tutoring services.

In sessions, Dr. Davis takes into account students’ neuropsychological profiles in order to capitalize on their strengths that, when tapped, can help overcome areas of weakness.

Sessions provide positive, successful learning experiences, which helps to improve students’ emotional functioning and self-confidence surrounding schoolwork.

Education

Dr. Davis received her Bachelor of Arts Degree with Honors in Education Studies from Brown University. As an undergraduate, she developed procedures to increase prosocial behaviors in typically developing children in inclusive preschool classrooms.

She also worked as a research assistant in the Brown University Timing Lab investigating how the temporal relationship between environmental cues and reinforcements can be used to predict the learning pattern and rate of behavior in animals.

Her fascination for basic and clinical research in the fields of psychology and education led her to pursue her Master of Science and Doctor of Psychology degrees under Dr. Barbara Gerson’s mentorship at Yeshiva University.

She held pre-doctoral clinical training appointments at the Churchill School and Center, New York Presbyterian-Columbia School Based Mental Health Program, New York Presbyterian-Columbia Department of Pediatric Oncology, and Weill Cornell Medical College Department of Neurosurgery. Her clinical work and research as a doctoral student focused on the neurobiological bases and treatment of learning disabilities.

Experience

Dr. Davis is currently a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the Cognitive Development and Neuroimaging Lab in the Columbia University Division of Child and Adolescent Psychiatry. At Columbia, her research investigates the neural correlates and biological bases of learning disabilities.

Dr. Davis also holds the position Lead Psychometrician at Branching Minds, an award-winning education technology startup that develops web-based tools that help parents and teachers identify, understand, and address students’ learning struggles.

At Branching Minds, Dr. Davis leverages research in cognitive and neurological sciences to help teachers and parents use observable information to determine why a student is struggling to learn and which supports would best serve the specific learner.


Dr. Davis is an active volunteer with Achilles International. She has served as a guide for disabled runners in many races, including two half marathons and two marathons.
